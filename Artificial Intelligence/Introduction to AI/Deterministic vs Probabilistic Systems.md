# Deterministic Systems
These follow strict pre-programmed rules to process data. They work in a flowchart-like manner; if condition A is met, then action B happens, etc.

These systems produce the same output when given identical input, making them predictable and reliable for structured tasks.

Limitations:
- Require clean, structured and consistently formatted data
- Cannot adapt to unexpected data variations or extreme cases
# Probabilistic Systems
These use statistical models and algorithms to make informed decisions based on patterns and probabilities.

Pros:
- Calculate the probability of different outcomes
- Handles uncertainty and ambiguity
- Works with messy, incomplete or inconsistent data
- Adapt processing based on statistical patterns
